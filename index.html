Dan Monte

Highland Park, NJ · (774) 319-1173· monted3@gmail.com

Dan obtained a Bachelor’s Degree from Ithaca College in 2018, where he studied percussion with Gordon Stout, Conrad Alexander, and Greg Evans. Here, he studied jazz harmony, arranging, and composition with Mike Titlebaum, Nick Weiser, and Gabe Condon. During this time he also made educational visits to Havana, Cuba to study Afro-Cuban popular and folkloric styles with Yaroldy Abreu Robles, Enrique Pla, Jean Roberto San Cristobal, Giraldo Piloto, Peruchin, among other greats. Dan continued his education by completing a Master’s Degree at Rutgers University in 2020, studying with Joseph Tompkins, Mike Truesdell, Kyle Zerna, Ian Sullivan, and Javier Diaz. Dan has performed in many arenas including: Off-Broadway, Popular, Orchestral/Opera, Ballet, Contemporary, Chamber, Jazz, World Fusion, Worship and others. Dan’s original compositions can be heard in “Fall Risk the Musical,” a musical comedy about living with disabilities and Cystic Fibrosis. Dan also writes music for and produces “The Move for Mind Podcast”, a podcast determined to promote dialogue about all aspects of mental health, movement, and their many intersections.
